Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM) Policy

Zero Tolerance: Votcen has a strict zero-tolerance policy against Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM).

Votcen is committed to maintaining a safe environment for all users, including candidates, voters, and administrators. We strictly prohibit the upload, sharing, storage, or distribution of content that depicts, promotes, or normalizes sexual abuse or exploitation of children.

1. Scope and Prohibited Content

This policy applies to all user-generated content on Votcen, including but not limited to:

  • Candidate Profiles: Profile photos, campaign banners, gallery images, and videos.
  • Support Requests: Content submitted via support forms or emails.

Prohibited Content (CSAM) includes:

  • Photos or videos depicting sexual acts involving minors.
  • Computer-generated imagery (CGI), realistic illustrations, or "deepfakes" of child sexual abuse.
  • Solicitation or grooming of minors for sexual purposes.
  • Links to external sites hosting CSAM.

2. Detection and Reporting

We employ a combination of automated technologies and human moderation to detect and remove Prohibited Content.

How to Report

If you encounter any content on Votcen that you believe violates this policy, please report it immediately:

  • Email: support@votcen.com
  • In-App: Use the "Report" feature available on profiles and content items.

3. Enforcement Actions

If CSAM is detected or reported, we will take immediate and decisive action:

  • Immediate Removal: The content will be permanently removed from our platform.
  • Account Termination: The responsible user account will be immediately and permanently banned.
  • Device Blocking: We may block the device associated with the account to prevent future access.
  • Preservation of Evidence: User data, logs, and content related to the violation will be preserved as required by law to assist investigations.

4. Law Enforcement Cooperation

In accordance with applicable laws (including 18 U.S.C. ยง 2258A), Votcen will report all apparent instances of CSAM to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and cooperate fully with law enforcement agencies in their investigations.

5. External Resources

If you or someone you know is a victim of child exploitation, help is available:

  • National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC)
  • RAINN (Rape, Abuse & Incest National Network)
